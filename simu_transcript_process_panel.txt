Demo setup
Topic: AI-assisted Medical Diagnosis
Researcher background: HCI researcher studying explainable AI
Expert background: Radiologist with 10 years experience

How to use
Enter each Researcher turn in the left input box and each Expert turn in the right input box.
The goal of this script is not perfect label control. It is to make ProcessGap visible while also giving the other gap types a fair chance to appear.

Turn 1
Researcher: When the AI flags a chest CT as abnormal, what do you usually look at first?
Expert: I do not start from the heatmap. I first check whether the case is in-distribution, then I look at the lesion candidate list, the calibrated malignancy score, and whether the model confidence is unstable across adjacent slices.
Expected dominant signal: LexicalGap or ConceptualGap

Turn 2
Researcher: So the heatmap is basically the model's reasoning process, right?
Expert: Not really. It is only a visual cue. It does not expose the full reasoning process and it definitely does not guarantee that the probability estimate is well calibrated.
Expected dominant signal: ConceptualGap

Turn 3
Researcher: When you say calibrated, do you mean the model is simply more accurate on easy cases?
Expert: Calibration means the predicted probability matches the real-world likelihood. A model can rank cases well and still be badly calibrated.
Expected dominant signal: LexicalGap

Turn 4
Researcher: In the interface, would changing the heatmap color from red to blue make you trust it more?
Expert: Color matters a little, but my bigger concern is where the AI fits in the clinical workflow, when I should ignore it, and how it affects reporting responsibility.
Expected dominant signal: ScopeGap

Turn 5
Researcher: Could you walk me through how you decide whether to accept the AI suggestion in an ambiguous case?
Expert: Often it comes down to tacit cues, like whether the finding pattern is anatomically plausible, whether the confidence matches the image quality, and whether the suggestion fits the patient's history.
Expected dominant signal: TacitGap

Turn 6
Researcher: Let's stay on confidence. If the confidence score is 0.62, what exactly goes through your mind?
Expert: I ask whether that number is calibrated for this subgroup, whether neighboring slices agree, and whether the model is unstable around the operating threshold.
Expected dominant signal: Process tracking should start to center on the confidence/calibration branch

Turn 7
Researcher: And if it is 0.58 instead of 0.62, how does your interpretation change?
Expert: The exact number matters less than whether the case sits near the decision threshold and whether that uncertainty changes the clinical consequence.
Expected dominant signal: repeated local focus on the same topic

Turn 8
Researcher: If it drops to 0.55, do you still think about it in the same way?
Expert: Yes. I still read it through threshold sensitivity, calibration, and what kind of false positive or false negative would follow.
Expected dominant signal: ProcessGap may begin to flag repeated_topic

Turn 9
Researcher: One more on confidence: what if it is 0.52?
Expert: Again, I would not treat that number alone as truth. I compare it with image quality, clinical priors, and slice consistency before acting on it.
Expected dominant signal: ProcessGap may escalate to repeated_topic or tunnel_vision

Turn 10
Researcher: Let me zoom out. Where in the workflow are you most likely to deliberately ignore the AI output?
Expert: Usually during handoff, when the case is out-of-distribution, when image quality is poor, or when the AI conflicts with the patient's broader context.
Expected dominant signal: the Process Guidance panel should show a branch shift away from the repeated confidence topic

Turn 11
Researcher: What kinds of handoff problems make the AI recommendation less useful?
Expert: If the next clinician does not know what evidence the AI relied on, or if responsibility is unclear, the recommendation becomes harder to use safely.
Expected dominant signal: ScopeGap or TacitGap

Turn 12
Researcher: If you had to teach a new radiologist one rule for using AI safely, what would it be?
Expert: Treat the AI as a second reader, not as an authority. Trust it more when it aligns with anatomy and workflow, and distrust it when confidence looks clean but context looks wrong.
Expected dominant signal: ConceptualGap or TacitGap

What to watch in the new Process Guidance panel
After turns 6 to 9, the Recent Topic Trail should keep showing closely related topics.
Coverage View should remain local to the current branch, not the whole interview.
If ProcessGap is triggered, the Latest Process Signal card should show a drift type and a redirect question that can be clicked to fill the researcher input.

Fallback if ProcessGap does not trigger strongly
Keep asking three or four more questions about the exact same confidence/calibration issue.
Avoid introducing new concepts like workflow, handoff, or patient communication until after the repeated-topic segment.
