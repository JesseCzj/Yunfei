Turn 3
Researcher: How do you typically evaluate if a virtual cell model is reliable? Which specific metrics matter?

Expected dominant signal: LexicalGap (Pearson correlation, log2FC, UMAP, scRNA-seq) or TacitGap (the "off" feeling that can't be formalized)

Turn 4
Researcher: What do you mean by off?

Expected dominant signal: TacitGap (gut feeling from experience, can't be formalized or measured, implicit domain intuition)

Turn 5
Researcher: Got it. Let's move to manual genetic perturbation workflows. Could you walk us through the key steps?

Turn 6
Researcher: I'm unfamiliar with ATAC-seq -- could you explain it briefly?
Expert: It's Assay for Transposase-Accessible Chromatin using sequencing. It shows which genome regions are open to transcription factors post-perturbation -- critical for confirming regulatory mechanisms alongside scRNA-seq.
Expected dominant signal: LexicalGap (explicit terminology confusion acknowledged by researcher)

Turn 7
Researcher: Have you used AI-based gene prediction models beyond Cellforge?

Expected dominant signal: ConceptualGap (non-intuitive gene interactions, high-throughput screening as a concept)

Turn 8

Expert: They're partially useful, but there's a disconnect. The VIS team's simplified visuals only label activation/inhibition, but we need epigenomic context -- like if the interaction is mediated by histone acetylation. Their focus on aesthetics and usability misses the biological granularity we need to validate predictions.
Expected dominant signal: ScopeGap (HCI team prioritizes simplicity/usability vs expert needs biological granularity and epigenomic context -- classic focus mismatch)

Turn 9

Turn 10

Turn 11
